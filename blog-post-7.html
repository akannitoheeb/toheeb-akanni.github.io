What Is Email Automation, Really?

Email automation is the process of sending pre-written emails to subscribers automatically, based on triggers and timing rules you define. Instead of manually writing and sending each email, you build a sequence once, set the conditions, and the system handles the rest.

Think of it like this: imagine you walk into a boutique in Lagos and the owner greets you warmly, asks what you're looking for, shows you relevant products, tells you about a special offer, and follows up a few days later to see if you're satisfied. Now imagine that entire experience happening in your subscriber's inbox — automatically, for every single person who joins your list — without you lifting a finger.

That is email automation. It is not spam. It is not impersonal. When done right, it is the most personal and effective marketing tool available to Nigerian businesses.

Step-by-Step: Build Your First Automated Email Sequence

Step 1

Map Your Customer Journey

Before you write a single email, you need to understand the path a stranger takes to become a paying customer. For most Nigerian businesses, that journey looks like this:

  • Awareness — they discover you (social media, Google search, referral)
  • Interest — they visit your website or social profile and want to learn more
  • Consideration — they compare you with alternatives and evaluate your offer
  • Decision — they are ready to buy but need a final push
  • Retention — they have bought and you want them to buy again

Your automated sequence should have emails that address each stage. A subscriber who just joined needs education and trust-building. A subscriber who clicked your pricing link needs a different message entirely.

Step 2

Choose Your Automation Trigger

A trigger is the event that starts your automated sequence. Brevo offers several powerful triggers, but for beginners, these three are the most effective:

  • Contact added to a list — the classic welcome sequence. Every new subscriber receives your pre-planned emails on schedule.
  • Contact clicks a link — perfect for interest-based sequences. If someone clicks your pricing page, trigger a sales-focused follow-up.
  • Contact makes a purchase — ideal for post-purchase sequences. Thank them, request a review, and upsell complementary services.

In Brevo, go to Automation → Create a Workflow → Select your trigger. Name your workflow clearly, like "Welcome Sequence — New Subscribers" or "Post-Purchase Follow-Up."

Step 3

Write Your Sequence Emails

Here is a proven 5-email welcome sequence that converts subscribers into customers for Nigerian businesses:

  • Email 1 — Immediate (Day 0): Deliver your lead magnet, introduce yourself, and set expectations. Subject: "Your [Lead Magnet] is inside, [First Name]"
  • Email 2 — Day 2: Share a personal story that connects your journey to their problem. Build emotional trust. Subject: "The mistake that cost me ₦200,000 (and how I fixed it)"
  • Email 3 — Day 4: Provide massive value — a tip, strategy, or insight they can use immediately. No selling yet. Subject: "The 3-minute hack that doubled my client inquiries"
  • Email 4 — Day 6: Introduce your product or service naturally. Show how it solves the problem you've been discussing. Include a soft CTA. Subject: "This is how I help businesses like yours [achieve result]"
  • Email 5 — Day 8: Address objections, share testimonials, and make a clear offer with urgency. Subject: "Still thinking it over? Here's what my clients say..."

Each email should be 200–400 words. Write like you're talking to one person over a bottle of malt in a Lagos cafe. Personal, warm, and direct.

Step 4

Set Timing and Delays

Timing is everything in email automation. Send too fast and you overwhelm subscribers. Send too slow and they forget who you are. Here is the timing framework that works for Nigerian audiences:

  • Welcome email: Immediate (0 minutes after subscription)
  • Educational emails: Every 2–3 days
  • Sales emails: Every 4–5 days, with increasing urgency
  • Re-engagement emails: After 30–60 days of no opens

In Brevo, add a "Wait" block between each email in your workflow. Set the delay to the number of days or hours you want. Test different timings and watch your open rates to find what works best for your audience.

Step 5

Add Personalisation and Conditional Logic

The most powerful automations don't send the same email to everyone. They adapt based on subscriber behaviour. Brevo allows you to add conditional blocks that split your sequence based on actions:

  • If subscriber opens Email 3 → send Email 4 (they're engaged)
  • If subscriber does not open Email 3 → send a re-engagement email with a different subject line
  • If subscriber clicks pricing link → move them to a sales-focused sequence
  • If subscriber makes a purchase → move them to a post-purchase nurture sequence

This is where automation becomes truly intelligent. Instead of broadcasting the same message to everyone, you are having personalised conversations at scale. One subscriber gets educational content. Another gets a limited-time offer. Both feel like you wrote that email just for them.

Step 6

Test, Launch, and Monitor

Before you activate your automation, test it thoroughly:

  • Add your own email address as a test contact and run through the entire sequence
  • Check that links work, images load, and personalisation tags populate correctly
  • Send a test to yourself and view it on both mobile and desktop
  • Ask a friend or colleague to review for clarity and tone

Once live, monitor these key metrics weekly:

  • Open rate: Aim for 20–30% for Nigerian audiences
  • Click-through rate: 2–5% is healthy for most industries
  • Unsubscribe rate: Keep below 0.5% per email
  • Conversion rate: Track how many subscribers become paying customers

Don't obsess over perfection. Launch your sequence, collect data, and improve over time. A good automation running today beats a perfect automation that never launches.

Step 7

Build Advanced Sequences Over Time

Once your welcome sequence is working, expand your automation system with these high-impact sequences:

  • Abandoned cart sequence — for ecommerce businesses. Remind customers to complete their purchase with a 3-email series.
  • Re-engagement sequence — for inactive subscribers. Win them back with a "We miss you" email and a special offer.
  • Onboarding sequence — for service businesses. Guide new clients through your process and set expectations.
  • Upsell sequence — after a purchase, introduce higher-tier products or services.
  • Referral sequence — ask satisfied customers to refer friends in exchange for a reward.

Each sequence takes 2–3 hours to build but runs indefinitely. That is the definition of working smart, not hard.

What Your Automated Email System Should Include

Based on my experience building automations for Nigerian businesses, here are the non-negotiable elements every system needs:

  • A clear entry point — every automation needs a specific trigger (signup, purchase, click, etc.)
  • Logical email flow — each email should build on the previous one, guiding the subscriber toward a specific goal
  • Personalisation tokens — use [First Name], [Company], or custom fields to make emails feel personal
  • Mobile-optimised design — over 70% of Nigerians read emails on mobile; test every email on a phone
  • A single call to action per email — confusion kills conversions; tell them exactly one thing to do next
  • Exit conditions — remove subscribers from a sequence when they complete the desired action (purchase, book a call, etc.)
  • Re-engagement rules — automatically tag or remove subscribers who haven't opened emails in 90 days
  • Analytics tracking — know your numbers so you can improve your sequences over time

Common Automation Mistakes to Avoid

I've made these mistakes. I've watched other Nigerian business owners make them too. Save yourself the frustration:

  • Setting it and forgetting it — automation is not a one-time task. Review your sequences monthly. Update offers, refresh testimonials, and remove broken links.
  • Too many emails too fast — sending 5 emails in 3 days will get you marked as spam. Respect your subscriber's inbox.
  • No personalisation — "Dear Customer" feels robotic. Use their name, reference their actions, and write like a human.
  • Selling too early — the first email should never be a sales pitch. Build trust first. Sell second.
  • Ignoring mobile optimisation — if your email looks terrible on a phone, you've lost most of your Nigerian audience
  • No clear goal for each sequence — every automation should have one specific outcome: a purchase, a booking, a reply. Know your goal before you build.
