How to Build an Email List from Zero: A Complete Guide for Nigerian Businesses

TA
Toheeb Akanni June 2026
⏱ 12 min read

When I started my digital marketing journey in Lagos, I made the same mistake most Nigerian business owners make: I focused entirely on social media and completely ignored email marketing. I was posting daily on Instagram, running Facebook ads, and chasing likes — while my competitors were quietly building email lists that generated consistent revenue month after month.

It took me losing a Facebook page with over 3,000 followers to a suspension to realise the truth: you don't own your social media audience. The algorithm decides who sees your content. The platform decides if your account stays open. But your email list? That belongs to you. Forever.

In this article, I'll show you exactly how to build an email list from zero — using free tools, proven strategies, and real examples that work specifically for Nigerian businesses.

Why Email Marketing Matters for Nigerian Businesses

Before we get into the how, let me explain why email marketing is not just important — it's essential for any Nigerian business that wants to grow sustainably.

Social media platforms in Nigeria are crowded, expensive, and unpredictable. A single algorithm change can reduce your reach from 10,000 people to 500 overnight. But email operates on a completely different principle. When someone gives you their email address, they are inviting you into their inbox. They're saying, "I want to hear from you." That is the most valuable permission a business can receive.

Consider this: the average Nigerian checks their email at least once per day. WhatsApp is personal. Instagram is entertainment. But email is where people go to make decisions — to review proposals, confirm appointments, and yes, to buy products and services. Email marketing delivers an average return of ₦42 for every ₦1 spent. No social media platform comes close to that.

More importantly, your email list is an asset you own. If Instagram shuts down tomorrow, your followers disappear. If Facebook suspends your ad account, your leads dry up. But your email list stays with you. You can export it, migrate it, and use it on any platform you choose. That is true business security.

Step-by-Step: How to Build Your Email List from Zero

Step 1

Choose Your Email Marketing Platform

The foundation of your email list is the platform you use to store contacts, send emails, and track results. For Nigerian businesses, I recommend Brevo for three reasons:

  • Free tier is generous — 300 emails per day, unlimited contacts, full automation
  • Nigerian-friendly — accepts Nigerian cards for upgrades, no restrictions on local businesses
  • Easy to use — drag-and-drop email builder, no technical skills required

Sign up at brevo.com, verify your account, and create your first contact list. Name it something clear like "Main Newsletter" or "Business Leads."

Step 2

Create an Irresistible Lead Magnet

People will not give you their email address for nothing. You need to offer something valuable in exchange — this is called a lead magnet. The best lead magnets solve a specific problem your target audience faces.

Here are lead magnet ideas that work well for Nigerian businesses:

  • For fashion businesses: "10 Outfit Combinations That Will Make You Look Expensive on Any Budget"
  • For real estate: "The Lagos Property Buyer's Checklist: 15 Questions to Ask Before You Pay"
  • For food businesses: "5 Secret Recipes That Will Keep Your Customers Coming Back"
  • For service providers: "The Exact Proposal Template That Won Me ₦500,000 in Contracts"
  • For coaches and consultants: "The 7-Day Action Plan to [Achieve Specific Result]"

Create your lead magnet using Canva (free). Make it a PDF, keep it under 10 pages, and ensure it delivers real value. A bad lead magnet will get downloads but no trust. A great lead magnet turns subscribers into customers.

Step 3

Build Your Email Capture Form

Now you need a way for people to actually subscribe. In Brevo, go to Contacts → Forms → Create a New Form. Choose an embedded form (not a popup, for now).

Your form should have exactly two fields:

  • First Name — so you can personalise your emails
  • Email Address — the only required field

Keep the form simple. Every extra field you add reduces conversions by approximately 11%. For the headline, use something benefit-driven like:

  • "Get My Free [Lead Magnet Name]"
  • "Join 500+ Business Owners Getting Weekly Marketing Tips"
  • "Download the Guide That Changed How I Approach [Topic]"

Add a privacy note below the button: "No spam. Unsubscribe anytime." This builds trust and increases sign-ups.

Step 4

Place Your Form Where People Will See It

A beautiful form hidden on page 7 of your website is useless. You need strategic placement. Here are the highest-converting locations for Nigerian businesses:

  • Website homepage — above the fold, with a clear headline and your lead magnet offer
  • About page — people who read your story are already interested; capture them there
  • Blog posts — add an inline form or a content upgrade (a bonus related to the article)
  • Contact page — offer your lead magnet as an alternative to direct contact
  • Social media bio link — use a simple landing page with your form as the only focus
  • WhatsApp status and broadcasts — share the link regularly with a compelling hook

If you don't have a website yet, create a free landing page using GitHub Pages or Carrd (free tier) and direct all your traffic there. One focused page will outperform a cluttered website every time.

Step 5

Set Up Your Welcome Email Automation

The moment someone subscribes is the moment they are most engaged. Don't waste it. Set up an automated welcome email that delivers your lead magnet and starts building a relationship.

Your welcome email should include:

  • A warm greeting using their first name — "Hi [First Name], welcome to the community!"
  • The promised lead magnet — attach the PDF or link to the download
  • A brief introduction — who you are, what you do, and why you started this list
  • What to expect — how often you'll email and what kind of value you'll share
  • A soft call to action — follow you on Instagram, reply to the email, or book a consultation

In Brevo, go to Automation → Create a Workflow → Welcome Message. Set the trigger to "Contact Added to a List" and the action to "Send an Email." This takes 10 minutes and runs forever.

Step 6

Drive Traffic to Your Sign-Up Form

Now that your system is set up, you need people to find it. Here are the most effective free strategies for Nigerian businesses:

  • WhatsApp Status — post daily value snippets with a CTA: "Want the full guide? Drop your email in my bio link."
  • Instagram Stories — use the "Link" sticker to direct followers to your sign-up page
  • LinkedIn posts — share insights and end with: "I break this down in my weekly newsletter. Link in comments."
  • Facebook Groups — provide genuine value in relevant groups, then invite interested members to subscribe
  • Guest appearances — when you're interviewed on podcasts or featured in articles, mention your newsletter
  • Referral incentives — offer a bonus to subscribers who refer a friend (e.g., "Share this with 3 friends and get my exclusive bonus guide")

The key is consistency. One post won't build a list. But one valuable post every day for 30 days will.

Step 7

Send Valuable Emails Consistently

Building the list is only half the battle. Keeping subscribers engaged is what turns them into customers. Commit to a regular email schedule — weekly is ideal, biweekly is acceptable, monthly is the minimum.

Every email you send should deliver one of these three things:

  • Education — teach something your audience doesn't know but needs to
  • Inspiration — share a story, case study, or transformation that motivates action
  • Entertainment — make them smile, laugh, or feel something genuine

A simple email structure that works:

  • Subject line — curiosity-driven or benefit-driven (e.g., "The ₦0 marketing strategy that doubled my clients")
  • Opening hook — a relatable story or surprising fact in the first two sentences
  • Main content — one clear idea, broken down simply, with actionable takeaways
  • Call to action — one specific thing you want them to do next
  • Sign-off — personal, warm, and consistent with your brand voice

Don't worry about being perfect. Worry about being consistent. Your first 10 emails will be your worst. Email 50 will be where you find your voice. Email 100 is where the magic happens.

What Your Email Marketing System Should Include

Based on my experience building lists for Nigerian businesses, here are the non-negotiable elements every email marketing system must have:

  • A clear value proposition — subscribers must know exactly what they'll get and why it matters
  • A professional sender name — use your real name or business name, not a generic noreply address
  • Mobile-optimised emails — over 70% of Nigerians read emails on their phones
  • A single call to action per email — confusion kills conversions; tell them exactly what to do next
  • Personalisation — use their first name, reference their location, or mention their industry
  • Segmentation — group subscribers by interest or behaviour so you send relevant content
  • A re-engagement strategy — after 90 days of no opens, send a "We miss you" email or remove them
  • Compliance — include an unsubscribe link in every email; it's required by law and builds trust

Common Mistakes to Avoid

I've made these mistakes. I've seen other Nigerian business owners make them too. Learn from us:

  • Buying email lists — it's illegal, destroys your sender reputation, and yields zero results. Build organically or don't build at all.
  • Sending only promotional emails — if every email is "Buy my product," people will unsubscribe. Lead with value, follow with offers.
  • Inconsistent sending — going silent for three months then blasting 5 emails in a week trains subscribers to ignore you
  • Ignoring subject lines — 47% of recipients open emails based on the subject line alone. Spend time crafting them.
  • No welcome sequence — a single welcome email is not enough. Build a 3-5 email sequence that nurtures new subscribers over their first two weeks.
  • Not tracking metrics — if you don't measure open rates, click rates, and unsubscribe rates, you can't improve

The Results You Can Expect

Here's what building an email list has done for my business and my clients:

  • My first 100 subscribers came within 30 days using only WhatsApp Status and Instagram
  • A fashion client generated ₦350,000 in sales from a single email to 400 subscribers
  • A real estate consultant booked 12 property viewings from one newsletter to 250 subscribers
  • My average open rate is 28% — nearly double the industry average for Nigerian businesses
  • Email consistently outperforms social media for direct sales by a factor of 5x

✅ You don't need a big budget, a large following, or years of experience to build a profitable email list. You need a valuable lead magnet, a simple sign-up form, consistent content, and the discipline to show up in your subscribers' inboxes week after week.

Your Next Step

If you're a Nigerian business owner without an email list, starting one today is the single highest-ROI marketing decision you can make. Social media followers are rented. Email subscribers are owned. The difference is the difference between a business that survives algorithm changes and one that doesn't.

Start today. Sign up for Brevo, create one lead magnet, build one form, and share it everywhere your audience already is. Your first subscriber is more valuable than your thousandth Instagram follower. Treat them that way.
