WordPress vs Custom Website: Which Is Better for Your Nigerian Business?

TA
Toheeb Akanni June 2026
⏱ 13 min read

Three months ago, a fashion business owner in Lekki called me in frustration. She had paid a developer ₦350,000 to build her a "custom website" — only to discover it was a basic WordPress template with a few colour changes. She felt cheated. She was cheated. And she is not alone.

Every week, Nigerian business owners ask me the same question: "Should I use WordPress or build a custom-coded website?" The answer is not as simple as most developers want you to believe. It depends on your business size, your budget, your technical skills, and — most importantly — what you actually need your website to do.

In this article, I'll break down both options honestly, using real numbers, real examples, and zero jargon. By the end, you'll know exactly which option is right for your business.

"The best website is not the most expensive one or the most technically advanced one. The best website is the one that helps your business make more money with less stress."

What Is WordPress, Really?

WordPress powers over 43% of all websites on the internet. It is a content management system — software that lets you build and manage a website without writing code. You install it on a hosting server, choose a theme (design template), add plugins for extra features, and publish your content through a visual editor.

For Nigerian businesses, WordPress is popular because it is accessible. You can find WordPress developers everywhere from Lagos to Kano. Themes cost between ₦15,000 and ₦80,000. Hosting on Nigerian providers like Whogohost or Qservers starts at ₦3,000 per year. With a small budget and basic guidance, you can have a functional website live within a week.

But WordPress has a dark side that few developers mention honestly. It is built on PHP, a programming language that requires regular updates. Plugins conflict with each other. Themes bloat your site with unnecessary code. And every additional feature — speed optimisation, security hardening, custom design — requires yet another plugin or paid add-on.

What Is a Custom-Coded Website?

A custom-coded website is built from scratch using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — the fundamental languages of the web. There is no WordPress engine running underneath. No plugins. No bloated theme files. Just clean, efficient code that does exactly what your business needs and nothing more.

My own portfolio website at toheebakanni.name.ng is custom-coded. It loads in under one second. It scores 99/100 on Google PageSpeed. It has zero plugin vulnerabilities because there are no plugins. And it costs me exactly ₦500 per year to host on GitHub Pages with a custom domain.

The trade-off is clear: custom websites require technical skill to build and modify. You cannot log into a dashboard and drag elements around. If you want to change something, you edit the code. For business owners who need frequent updates — daily blog posts, changing prices, new product listings — this can be a limitation unless you have a developer on retainer or learn basic HTML.

When WordPress Is the Right Choice

Despite its drawbacks, WordPress is the correct choice for many Nigerian businesses. Choose WordPress if:

  • You need a blog or content-heavy site — WordPress was built for publishing. If you plan to post articles, news, or updates multiple times per week, the dashboard makes this effortless.
  • You run an ecommerce store — WooCommerce (a WordPress plugin) is the most mature ecommerce solution for small businesses. It handles payments, inventory, shipping, and taxes.
  • You need frequent content updates — if your team changes prices, adds services, or updates galleries regularly, WordPress lets non-technical staff make changes without calling a developer.
  • You want to manage the site yourself — the WordPress dashboard is intuitive. With basic training, you or a team member can handle day-to-day updates.
  • You need specific functionality quickly — there is a plugin for almost everything: booking systems, membership areas, forums, multilingual support. You install it and it works.

I recommend WordPress for businesses like restaurants with changing menus, real estate agencies with new listings, news blogs, and ecommerce stores with large product catalogues. The convenience outweighs the performance trade-offs for these use cases.

When a Custom Website Is the Right Choice

Choose a custom-coded website if:

  • Speed and performance are priorities — Google uses page speed as a ranking factor. A slow WordPress site can cost you search visibility. Custom sites are inherently fast because they contain only the code you need.
  • You have a limited budget for ongoing costs — custom sites hosted on GitHub Pages cost ₦500 per year. WordPress hosting, security plugins, backup services, and premium themes add up quickly.
  • You want a completely unique design — WordPress themes constrain your layout. Custom code gives you total creative freedom. Every pixel can be exactly how you envision it.
  • Security is critical — WordPress sites are attacked constantly because they are common targets. Custom sites have no known vulnerabilities because there is no standardised system to exploit.
  • You have a static or semi-static site — portfolio websites, landing pages, service business sites, and personal brands don't need constant dashboard updates. Custom code is perfect here.
  • You want to rank on Google — clean HTML with proper semantic structure, fast load times, and zero bloat gives custom sites a significant SEO advantage over plugin-heavy WordPress installations.

I recommend custom-coded websites for freelancers, consultants, agencies, personal brands, law firms, architects, and any business where the website's primary job is to impress visitors and convert them into leads — not to manage dynamic content.

Real Costs for Nigerian Businesses

Let me be transparent about what each option actually costs in Nigeria today:

WordPress (Year 1):

  • Domain (.com.ng): ₦5,000
  • Hosting (shared): ₦12,000
  • Premium theme: ₦25,000
  • Essential plugins: ₦15,000
  • Developer setup: ₦80,000
  • Total Year 1: ₦137,000
  • Annual recurring: ₦32,000+ (hosting, renewals, plugin subscriptions)

Custom-Coded (Year 1):

  • Domain (.name.ng): ₦500
  • Hosting (GitHub Pages): ₦0
  • Developer build: ₦150,000 – ₦300,000 (or free if you use AI + DIY)
  • Total Year 1: ₦150,500 – ₦300,500
  • Annual recurring: ₦500 (just the domain)

The custom site costs more upfront but saves you ₦30,000+ every single year after. Over five years, the custom site is significantly cheaper. And if you use AI tools like Claude to write the code and GitHub Pages for hosting, your total cost drops to just ₦500 for the domain.

The Hidden Cost Nobody Talks About

There is a cost that does not appear on any invoice: time and stress.

WordPress sites break. Plugins stop working after updates. Themes conflict with new WordPress versions. Your site goes down on a Sunday evening and your developer is not picking up calls. You spend hours troubleshooting, updating, and patching — time you should spend running your business.

My custom-coded website has required zero maintenance in six months. Zero updates. Zero broken features. Zero panic messages at midnight. It just works. For a business owner juggling staff, inventory, customers, and finances, that peace of mind is worth more than any hosting fee.

Can You Build a Custom Site Yourself?

Yes. And it is easier than you think.

I built my first custom website with zero coding knowledge. I used Claude AI to generate the HTML and CSS, copied the code into files, and uploaded them to GitHub Pages. The entire process took one weekend. My website now ranks on Google, loads in under a second, and costs me less than a bottle of water per month to maintain.

If you are comfortable with technology, willing to learn, and have 2–3 days to invest, building a custom site yourself is not only possible — it is the smartest financial decision you can make for your online presence.

If you are not technical and have no interest in learning, hire a developer who specialises in custom code. Be specific about what you want. Ask to see their previous custom work — not WordPress sites dressed up as custom. And never pay more than ₦300,000 for a basic business website, custom or not.

My Recommendation for Nigerian Business Owners

Here is my honest, no-nonsense advice based on the hundreds of businesses I have worked with:

  • Startups and solopreneurs with tight budgets: Build a custom site yourself using AI and free hosting. Total cost: ₦500. Total control: 100%.
  • Service businesses (consultants, agencies, creatives): Custom-coded portfolio or service site. Speed and uniqueness matter more than content management.
  • Ecommerce businesses: WordPress with WooCommerce. The inventory and payment integration features are unmatched for small Nigerian stores.
  • Content creators and bloggers: WordPress. The publishing workflow is designed for frequent content updates.
  • Established businesses with marketing budgets: Custom-coded site with a headless CMS for content updates. Best of both worlds — speed plus flexibility.

✅ The question is not "Which is better?" The question is "Which is better for my specific business, right now?" A ₦500 custom site that converts visitors into customers is infinitely better than a ₦200,000 WordPress site that sits idle because nobody can find it on Google.

Your Next Step

Stop overthinking your website decision. The worst choice is no choice at all. Every day without a professional website is a day potential clients cannot find you, cannot evaluate you, and cannot trust you enough to pay you.

If you sell products online, go with WordPress. If you sell services, build a custom site. If you have no budget and no technical skills, use AI to build a custom site for ₦500. If you have a budget and want the best of everything, hire a developer who understands both platforms and can recommend the right tool for your goals.

The platform does not build your business. You do. Pick one, build it, and move on to the work that actually grows your revenue.
